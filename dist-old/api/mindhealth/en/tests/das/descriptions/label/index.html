A negative score indicates that a person treats things like success, love and happiness as absolute necessities. They expect other people — and even the universe — to welcome them with open arms because of their personal qualities or achievements.

These expectations are often disappointed, which can lead to depression and related problems. Such people spend a lot of energy on worry, resentment and sadness, and large parts of their life start to feel unpleasant and painful.

They tend to complain but do little to actually resolve their difficulties, which ultimately lowers their overall satisfaction with life.

A positive score suggests that a person does not believe they were born for certain special privileges. They do their best to reach their goals and are often successful. They understand that every person is unique and that there is no reason for fate to revolve around their wishes.

Setbacks are seen as manageable disappointments rather than disasters. They do not expect perfection or fairness in everything and at all times. Thanks to patience, persistence and the ability to tolerate frustration, they often move ahead of others.
