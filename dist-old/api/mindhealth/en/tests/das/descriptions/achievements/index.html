A negative score indicates a dependence on work, where a person views themselves as a product on the job market. The lower the score, the more their joy and capacity for happiness depend on professional achievements.

If such a person goes on holiday with unfinished tasks, retires, or faces illness that prevents them from working, they are at high risk of emotional breakdown. Financial setbacks and emotional shocks are experienced as equally devastating.

A positive score, by contrast, shows that a person finds satisfaction in work but does not regard it as their only source of self-respect and happiness.
