The “Autonomy” scale assesses a person’s ability to find joy within themselves. A positive score shows that their moods are shaped mainly by their own thoughts and attitudes. Such a person recognizes responsibility for their feelings, understanding that these experiences arise primarily from their inner world.

They can feel not truly alone, because all their experiences are generated in their own mind. This allows them to explore and appreciate a rich, fascinating inner world.

A negative score, by contrast, indicates that a person believes joy and satisfaction come mainly from the outside world. This is a major disadvantage, because the external world is beyond their control. As a result, their mood becomes completely dependent on external circumstances.
