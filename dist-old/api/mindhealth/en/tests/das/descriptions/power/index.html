The “All-Powerfulness” scale reflects a person’s tendency to see themselves as the centre of the universe and to assume responsibility for everything that happens. A negative score points to a cognitive distortion in which a person unreasonably takes on responsibility for events beyond their control. They blame themselves for other people’s behaviour, feel guilty and self-condemning, which leaves them psychologically helpless.

A positive score, on the contrary, shows that a person understands they are not the “centre of the universe” and are not responsible for everyone and everything. This perspective does not distance them from others — it actually helps build healthier relationships.

They relate to others as equals and are not afraid of disagreement. Their fair attitude attracts people, as they do not try to control them. Others are more willing to listen to their ideas because they do not force their opinions on anyone.

They do not chase power, yet people are often ready to entrust it to them. Their relationships with close ones are based on mutuality rather than dependence. As a result, they earn love and respect without trying to dominate.
